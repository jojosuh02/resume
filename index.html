My Story

I have always strived for greatness: serving as senior class president in high school, holding two executive board positions in Phi Gamma Delta, competing in rowing and water polo, and earning first chair in orchestra for cello.

I learned the hard way that nothing is promised and progress is paid for upfront. I have worked in places where pressure is real, as an ocean lifeguard for Laguna Beach, a phlebotomist, and in emergency departments as a bedside scribe, with hospital experience across the world, in roles where hesitation costs lives.

When money ran out, pride went with it. I donated plasma twice a week, drove DoorDash and Uber Eats at the same time, and kept moving because stopping was not an option. By the age of 22, I had built five businesses that failed and carried tens of thousands of dollars in debt, lessons that cut deeper than any success.

Those years did not break me. They hardened my discipline, sharpened my judgment, and proved that endurance beats talent when things get ugly. I build practical solutions through biomedical innovation, 3D printing, and emerging AI tools, and I live by structure and routine to stay ready.

I believe effort compounds, setbacks are tuition, and if you keep showing up and executing when it is hardest, you earn your way forward.
